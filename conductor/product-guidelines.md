# Product Guidelines: Chill Flight

## 1. Tone and Voice: Atmospheric & Minimal
- **Brevity is Key**: Use minimal text in the UI to maintain a calm and uncluttered experience.
- **Evocative Language**: When text is used (e.g., in the loading screen or cockpit), choose words that evoke a sense of peacefulness and discovery.
- **Subtle Feedback**: Avoid intrusive or loud UI notifications. Prefer gentle visual or haptic feedback.

## 2. Visual Branding: Calm & Serene
- **Color Palette**: Utilize soft, pastel, or natural color schemes that reinforce a sense of relaxation and peace.
- **Geometry**: Prioritize clean, low-poly shapes with smooth transitions to minimize visual noise.
- **Atmospheric Effects**: Leverage sky colors, lighting, and environmental effects (like sun/moon cycles) to set the emotional tone.

## 3. Interaction Design: Deep Immersion
- **Minimalist HUD**: The cockpit and UI should be as non-intrusive as possible, keeping the player's focus on the flight and terrain.
- **Seamless Transitions**: Ensure smooth transitions between game states (e.g., from loading to flight) to avoid breaking the player's flow.
- **Intuitive Controls**: Flight mechanics should feel natural and rewarding without requiring complex tutorials.

## 4. Development Style: Readability First
- **Clean Code**: Prioritize code clarity and descriptive naming conventions to make the project accessible for collaboration.
- **Well-Commented**: Use meaningful comments to explain complex logic, especially for terrain generation and multiplayer synchronization.
- **Consistency**: Adhere to established patterns for Three.js and Firebase integration to ensure a coherent codebase.
- **Mobile Efficiency**: While prioritizing readability, maintain awareness of mobile performance constraints (e.g., avoiding unnecessary garbage collection in the game loop).
